According to historians, kayak means "boat man", since it only fits one person. The truth is that kayaking in Peru, and especially in Cusco, is a very common and rewarding sporting activity. For example, you can go to the Huaypo lagoon and slide through the placidity of its waters while you watch the local fishermen cast their nets to catch rainbow trout. Or if you prefer, you can go to the Piuray lagoon, in Chincheros, where the reflection of the valley's mountains will be your great company.

Amazing network of roads that connected the Inca Empire. The Inca Trail was the most sophisticated communication system that allowed managing resources and unifying the Inca empire. Walking the Inca Trail today, in Cusco, is a challenging and satisfying experience. For one or several days you will push yourself to the maximum walking at different heights enjoying beautiful landscapes. Lares, Ausangate, Salkantay, are some of the routes that are waiting for you.

The Sacred Valley of the Incas is a beautiful and popular place to practice paragliding. There are several companies that offer paragliding flights in the Sacred Valley of the Incas. Some of them are World Explorer1, Valle Sagrado VIP2, Dos Manos Peru3 and Civitatis4. You can choose the one that best suits your needs and budget.
